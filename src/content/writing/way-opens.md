Sit with me
Sink into the time-worn seams of the   
squashiest, brownest couch in the place  
Once, a snake fell from these rafters.  
Hand me that book on the shelf, say  
you read King Arthur as a kid?  
I did too. 

Sit with me   
Don’t even try to get comfortable   
on the splinters and staples  
of this picnic bench  
out and up in the mountains of the east,   
or the south, or wherever we are.   
Hand me the tiny scissors from the first aid kit  
and your empty journal pages, all folded up.   
Make me a snowflake, and another, and another  
and I’ll give you the ones I made for you. 

Sit with me  
Backs against this log we found  
Wait a second, for me to reach  
through spotty slices of shade   
for our gallon of water.   
This far in, it’s not cold or hot,  
just weight.   
We’re surrounded by miles  
in every direction but for us,  
there is only forward and back. 

Sit with me  
Many have walked this ground before  
but as the light fades, this dirt becomes sacred,  
and there is only us.   
Pass me, carefully, the pot of pasta and peanut butter,  
the stuff of life.  
The leaves above become a ghostly blur  
as daylight slides into the deepest dusk.  
Heat of the day loiters in the air  
as crickets reclaim the night,   
and a heaviness I didn’t know I carried   
is gone from my body.   
Only sitting here, in the night,   
do I notice its absence.
